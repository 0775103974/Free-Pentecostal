MISSION STATEMENT

The primary purpose of the Free pentecostal college is to provide a learning environmentin which faculty, staffs and students can discover, examine critically, preserve and transmit the knowledge , wisdom and values that will help ensure the survival of this and future generations and improve the quality of life for all. The university seeks to help students to develop an undeerstanding and appreciation for the complex cultural and physical world in which they live and to realize their highest poential of intellectual, physical and human development.

It seeks to attract and serve students from diverse social, economic and ethnic backgrounds and to be sensitive and responsive to those groups which has been underserved by higher education. To fulfill its mission, the college must:

  1. Offer broad and balanced academic programs that are mutually reinforcing and emphasize high quality and creative instruction at the undergraduate, graduate, professional and postgraduate levels.
  2. Generate new knowledge through a broad array of scholary, research and creative endeavors, which provide a foundation for dealing with the immediate and long-range needs of society.
  3. Achieve leadership in each discipline, strengthen interdisciplinary studies, and pioneer new fields of learning.
  4. Serve society through coordinated statewide outreach programs that meet continuing educational needs in accordance with the college designated land- grant status.
  5. Participate extnsively instatewide, national and international programs and encourage others in the Free pentecostal college system, at other educational institutions and in state, national and international organizations to seek benefit from the college unique educational resources, such as faculty and staff expertise, libraries, archieves, museums and research facilities.
  6. Strenghten cultural undeerstanding through opportunities to study languages, cultures, the arts and the implications of social, political, enconomic and technological change and through encouragement of study.
  7. Maintain a level of excellence and standards in all programs that will give them statewide, national and international significance.
  8. Embody, through its policies and programs, respect for, committment to, the ideals of a pluralistic, multiracial, open and democratic society.
